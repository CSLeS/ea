We're dedicated to helping LSE students answer one simple question:
How can we use our resources to do the most good?
Rather than just doing what feels right, Effective Altruism aims to use rigorous evidence and careful analysis to find the best ways to tackle the world's most pressing problems.
What is Effective Altruism?
Most of us want to make a difference. We see suffering, injustice and death, and are moved to do something about them. But working out what that ‘something’ is, let alone actually doing it, can be a difficult and disheartening challenge.
Effective Altruism is a response to this challenge. It is a research field which uses high-quality evidence and careful reasoning to work out how to help others as much as possible. It is also a community of people taking these answers seriously, by focusing their efforts on the most promising solutions to the world's most pressing problems.
How, then, can we figure out which causes to focus on? Researchers have found the following framework useful. Working on a cause is likely to be highly impactful when it is:
  • Great in scale (it affects many lives, by a great amount)
  • Highly neglected (few other people are working on addressing the problem)
  • Highly solvable or tractable (additional resources will do a great deal to address it).
On this basis, several cause areas have emerged as especially likely to be highly impactful.
Effective Altruism at LSE
As a society, we run regular workshops on how to have a large impact during your career, as well as socials, high-profile speaker events, free books, discussion groups, research opportunities, and Fellowships.
We welcome anyone; whether you're a postgraduate doing research on global priorities, or someone completely new to the concept! Besides our socials, Fellowships, and events, we offer coffee chats or walks with a committee member to introduce interested students to how they can get involved with the wider community, discuss career goals, decide which global problems to work on, or anything else!
